I am an open source software developer interested in the intersection of mathematics, statistics, and high performance computing.

My formal background is mathematics, with a hefty sprinkling of statistics mixed in. But these days I mostly look like a computer scientist. I know a great deal about the inner cobwebs of the R language, and spend a lot of my time linking it to high performance C and Fortran code.

I am founding member and co-lead developer of the pbdR project, which elevates the R programming language to supercomputers. Want to use R with 50,000 cores on a supercomputer? We can show you how.
