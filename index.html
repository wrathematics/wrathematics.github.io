I am an open source software developer interested in the intersection of mathematics, statistics, and high performance computing.

My formal background is mathematics, with a hefty sprinkling of statistics mixed in. But these days I mostly look like a computer scientist. I spent several years teaching, a year as a student working as a statistics consultant, but these days I work in high performance computing. I know a great deal about the inner cobwebs of the R language, and spend a lot of my time linking it to high performance C and Fortran code.

I am founding member and co-lead developer of the pbdR project. We do stuff with R on supercomputers, like fitting a linear model with 50,000 cores (it's pretty neat).
