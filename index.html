About

I am an open source software developer largely interested in the intersection of mathematics, statistics, and high performance computing.

My formal background is mathematics, with some sprinkling of statistics. But these days I mostly look like a computer scientist. I know a great deal about the inner cobwebs of the R language, and mostly spend my time linking it to high performance C and Fortran code.

Currently, I am founding member and co-lead developer of the pbdR project, which elevates the R programming language to supercomputers.
